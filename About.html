Luigi Lombardo

About Me

I am assistant professor of data driven modelling at the University of Twente, Faculty of Geo-Information Science and Earth Observation. I am a Spatio-temporal data-driven and physics-based modeling enthusiast. With main expertise in earth surface processes (e.g., landslides, flash floods, soil erosion).

Current scientific direction: understanding compound hazards’ dynamics, where climatic and/or seismic stresses induce chains of catastrophic effects. 
Long term goal: revolutionizing geomorphological risk management tools and policies.

Current PhD Students

Ashok Dahal

AI in Co-Seismic Landslides

Ashok is a geomatics engineer whose multidisciplinary work combines remote sensing, artificial intelligence and geophysics. He generates physics-based ground motion simulations, whose results are passed to a number of data driven models to infer on the earthquake influence on coseismic slope failures. 

Mateo Moreno Zapata

Spatio-Temporal modelling 

Mateo is a engineering geologist who focuses on modeling rainfall-triggered landslides and on how their associated hazard changes dynamically both through time and space. These considerations are achieved by exploring advanced space-time statistics in combination to radar precipitation products.

Letizia Elia

Cryospheric Hazards

Letizia is a physicist who works on cryospheric hazards induced by permafrost degradation via data-driven models. Her work examines peri-glacial environments and seeks for statistical dependences between cryospheric hazards and climate changes.

Zhice Fang

Space-time Modelling 

Zhice Fang is a geomatic engineer who makes use of space-time statistics and machine learning tools to examine long time series of landslides that occurred over large (regional scale) landscapes. He models the persistence of surface processes that can induce risk to human settlements and infrastructure.

Yuanjing Chen

Physics Based Modelling

Yuanjing Chen is a geotechnical engineer interested in adapting the Fibre Bundle Modeling approach to the space-time context and specifically for coseismic slope failures. His work involves linking physics-based ground motion simulations to physics-based landslide simulations.

Kun He

InSAR in Landslide Hazards

Kun He is an engineering geologist who explores the potential of InSAR analyses to characterize slope deformations in space and time. He empirically and “data-drivenly” assesses whether legacy effects of earthquakes or earthquake sequences increase the estimated deformation rates.

Tengfei Wang

AI in Deformation Timeseries

Tengfei Wang is an engineering geologist who works on examining time series of slow slope deformations estimated via field instruments such as permanent GPS stations and inclinometers. He then makes use of AI to model the spatio-temporal behavior of large landslides as a function of the climatic conditions they are exposed to.

Mahnoor Ahmed

Spatio-temporal Early Warning

Mahnoor Ahmed is a physical geographer whose research aims at combining space-time statistics, remote sensing and geomorphology for early-warning systems. These are implemented directly via cloud computing technologies to maximizes their spread and use for a wide audience.

Priscilla Niyokwiringirwa

Costal Landscape Evolution

Priscilla Niyokwiringirwa is a working on a multidisciplinary project where coastal landscape evolution is examined through data-driven and physics-based models. Her doctoral project involves evaluation of uncertainty propagation and field surveying for data collection.

Erin Bryce

Statstical Modelling

Erin Bryce is a statistician working on Log-Gaussian Cox Processes in the context of natural hazard modeling. She combines such complex geostatistical tools together with GIS technologies aiming at producing interpretable probabilistic estimates of compound hazard occurrences across the UK.
